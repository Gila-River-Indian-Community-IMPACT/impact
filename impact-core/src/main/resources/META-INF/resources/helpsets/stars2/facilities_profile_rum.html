Undelivered Mail

Overview 

The system’s Undelivered Mail page, available from the third-level menu of the Facilities tab, allows you to view a summary datagrid of undelivered mail associated with a facility.  When mail is returned Central Office staff will enter a record of the Undelivered Mail.  Generating this record will initiate a workflow.  If Central Office is unable to resolve the delivery problem the workflow will progress to the DO/LAA person assigned to the facility so that information can be obtained in order to allow the document to be re-delivered.

Undelivered Mail List

The following datagrid of information will appear when you access the third-level menu, Undelivered Mail:


  • ID# - Unique identification number assigned by the system when the record is created.  A hyperlink for viewing the details of the record and editing the record (discussed below).
  • Mailing Date – The postmark date of the document that was returned as undeliverable.
  • Category – The type of Undelivered Mail; for example, Invoice or PER.
  • Disposition – The status of the undelivered document.  Undelivered is identified at entry while after resolving either Completed – Resent or Completed – Not Resent will be identified.
  • Undeliverable Contact/Address – The addressee’s name and physical address, as identified on the package, where the undelivered document was originally mailed.
  • Reason for Return – The reason the document was returned, typically as identified on the package when returned to the sender.  For example, Refused or No Mail Receptacle.
  • Submitted By – The name of the person whom entered the record.
  • Comments – Relevant Comments entered by the submitter of the record.
  • Date Submitted – The date the record was entered into the system by the submitter.

Creating an Undelivered Mail Record

Typically only Central Office staff will create a record.  To create a new record clickand the following pop-up will appear:
  • Mailing Date – Enter the postmark date of the document that was returned as undeliverable.
  • Disposition – Select from the pick list the status of the undelivered document.  Undelivered is identified at entry while after resolving either Completed – Resent or Completed – Not Resent will be identified.
  • Category – Select from the pick list the type of Undelivered Mail; for example, Invoice or PER. If the type is not available, select another type and indicate as such in the Comments field.
  • Undeliverable Contact/Address – Enter the addressee’s name and physical address, as identified on the package, where the undelivered document was originally mailed.
  • Reason for Return – Indicate the reason the document was returned, typically as identified on the package when returned to the sender.  For example, Refused or No Mail Receptacle.
  • Comments – Enter any relevant Comments.

Resolving Undelivered Mail

Creating a record will generate a workflow. If Central Office is unable to resolve the delivery problem the workflow will progress to the DO/LAA person assigned to the facility so that information can be obtained in order to allow the document to be re-delivered.  The Undelivered Mail task will be identified in the person’s ToDos Home tab list.  The person resolving the record should locate the appropriate delivery information and update the information in the third-level menu Owner/Contact of the facility’s Facility Profile.  See the Owner/Contact Help page for assistance with editing these fields.  Once completed, the person resolving the record should complete sign-off of the workflow, identifying via the radio buttons on the Task Profile if the Facility Profile was updated. As a final step in resolving the record, Central Office staff will update the Undelivered Mail record’s Disposition to indicate the action taken.

For additional help with workflows or the Task Profile, see the Workflow Diagram and Task Profile Help pages.
